For years people have been trying to define the perfect diet for everyone, without any real success. This is because what is healthy for you completely depends on your DNA, your lifestyle and your microbiome composition. By using our platform you will be able to find out exactly what food is healthy for your body based on your blood glucose responses to what you eat. This can help you:

  • Lose fat
  • Feel more healthy and energetic
  • Increase your mental performance
  • Avoid diseases like diabetes or cardiovascular disease

How does it work?

Through our platform you are able to precisely track what you eat and at what time you started your meal (which is important for our analysis). Besides tracking your food intake you will also track your blood glucose response to a meal. When you are measuring blood glucose it is recommended that you schedule your meals in such a way that there is at least 2 hours between each meal (preferably 3 hours) as this will make the effect of each meal more clear. Also we recommend of making a list of meals that you want to test including:
  • Meals that you eat frequently (incorporating everything that would be included in the meal).
  • Food that you enjoy but avoid because you think it is unhealthy (you might find out it is not as unhealthy as you think).
  • Food that you don't enjoy as much but eat because you think it is healthy (you might find out it is not as healthy as you think).
For measuring your blood glucose response we recommend using a continuous blood glucose monitoring sensor such as the Freestyle Libre. However, it is also possible to use finger-pricking right before consuming a meal and then at 30, 60, 90 and 120 minutes after this meal.

The Science

Research has shown that the blood glucose response to a certain meal is an important indicator of health, having a higher blood glucose peak after a meal means that you will store fat and experience decreased physical as well as cognitive performance and in the long-term increase your chance on diseases such as diabetes type 2 , cardiovascular disease and obesity . It can also result in a overproduction of insulin leading to a blood glucose dip a few hours after your meal, which may cause overeating.

{% endblocktrans %}
{% blocktrans %}

Research has shown that people can have very different blood glucose responses to the same meal, based on their genetics, lifestyle and their gut microbiome composition . That's why it is important to measure our actual blood glucose responses to certain meals giving us insight in what food is healthy for you and what food you might want to avoid.
